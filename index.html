Welcome to Turkmenistan

Turkmenistan, located in Central Asia, is known for its deserts, ancient cities, and unique culture. Explore its geography, traditions, food, and top attractions below.

Geography

Turkmenistan is dominated by the Karakum Desert, with the Caspian Sea to the west. The country experiences an arid climate with hot summers and cold winters. Its landscapes include deserts, mountains, and fertile valleys along rivers.

Culture

Turkmen culture is rooted in nomadic traditions, horse breeding, and carpet weaving. The Turkmen language is widely spoken, with Russian also used in urban areas. Festivals often celebrate music, dance, and the famous Akhal-Teke horses.

Cuisine

  • Plov: Rice dish with lamb and vegetables.
  • Shurpa: Traditional meat and vegetable soup.
  • Chorek: Round bread baked in clay ovens.
  • Manty: Steamed dumplings filled with meat.
  • Kebab: Grilled skewers of lamb or beef.

Top 5 Must-Visit Attractions

Darvaza Gas Crater

Known as the “Door to Hell,” this fiery crater has burned for decades and is a unique natural wonder.

Ashgabat

The capital city, famous for its white marble buildings, modern architecture, and grand monuments.

Ancient Merv

A UNESCO World Heritage site showcasing ruins of one of the oldest cities in Central Asia.

Karakum Desert

Experience camel rides, nomadic traditions, and the vast desert landscape.

Kow Ata Underground Lake

A natural thermal lake inside a cave, perfect for relaxation and exploration.
